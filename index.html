About the job

Job summary

This is an outstanding opportunity offered within 11 Sqn, and is varied, rewarding and exciting! Do you have the passion to deliver maritime support within 11Sqn on a variety of small craft? Are you a pro-active, hard-working individual? One who is motivated to play an engaging, interesting and critical part in our support to Navy Command’s role to generate and operate forces for today and to ensure we are ready for tomorrow by developing and delivering the Navy of the future.

In order to deliver its outputs, 11 Squadron relies on an adaptable team with a good understanding of the customers’ requirements; the successful candidate will be expected to demonstrate at interview an ability to fit in and collaborate as part of this team. Timing of 11 Squadron's activities is constrained by tidal conditions which means that there will be requirements to work outside normal hours for which a premium will be paid in line with MOD policy.

Continuous Amphibious Readiness is one of the Royal Navy’s key outputs. 47 Commando Raiding Group Royal Marines (47Cdo (RG) RM) delivers support to the Commando Force’s amphibious capability. Within 47Cdo, 11 Squadron, based at RM Instow, enables this with the trials and development of amphibious equipment that is at the forefront of Future Commando Force, and delivering specialist amphibious training on a variety of vehicles and boats to project and sustain forces from the sea. The successful candidate will work as part of Zeta Berth crew, a small team delivering maritime support on a variety of military craft ranging from Rigid Inflatable Boats (RIB) to the Landing Craft Utility (LCU), that support the UK Amphibious capability by delivering training to RM students.

Much of 11 Squadron's work is experimental in nature, therefore there will be opportunities to participate in innovative trials and development activity.

Depending upon experience and qualifications, the successful applicant may be required to travel and stay overnight in order to complete training vital to fulfil the role.

This position is advertised at 37 hours per week.

Job description

You will be responsible for maintaining all of our craft in a safe and seaworthy condition to enable our Training and Trials output. This will involve working on the various mechanical, electrical and hydraulic systems including propulsion and navigation that are all essential for the safe operation of the craft. You will also be part of the boat crew, occasionally during unsocial hours, for which a premium will be paid, in accordance with current MOD policy. You will be part of a small team at Zeta Berth, working in an environment that supports creative thinking to resolve problems. You will also work closely with the Marine Engineering Officers at RM Tamar, the Zeta Berth Craft Maintenance Supervisor, who will support with taskings and the audit of maintenance records.

Main responsibilities

  • Maintain the safe and effective operation of 11sqn landing craft and other small boats at Zeta Berth.
  • Ensure all maritime activity is conducted in accordance with the appropriate hand books, instructions and sound engineering practice.
  • Support the Bosun in Command (BIC) with the safety of all passengers and crew aboard the craft while afloat.

This role requires a degree of physical ability, as it involves loading and unloading of goods and other manual handling tasks. Some of the work undertaken will be inside the engine room of the craft with restricted access and or confined spaces.

Other Responsibilities

  • Support the BIC with the security, safety and maintenance of the berthing facility.
  • Support 47Cdo with marine engineering taskings at HMNB Devonport.

Person specification

Working closely with the Craft Maintenance Supervisor, you will be responsible for the safe and efficient running of the Craft moored at Zeta Berth, which supports Defence amphibious training at RM Instow. You will be required to maintain the various craft operating systems in accordance with relevant policies and procedures, applying sound engineering principles. You will need to demonstrate an ability to work as part of a small team, and also use your initiative to solve problems to ensure the craft can operate safely.

Experience

  • Working on a wide range of Maritime craft platforms – Essential
  • Basic understanding of maritime environmental aspects and management – Desirable
  • Basic understanding of Microsoft O365 – Desirable

Essential
